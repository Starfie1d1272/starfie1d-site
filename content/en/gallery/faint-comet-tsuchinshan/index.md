The apparent magnitude of this comet had dropped significantly at the time of capture, rendering it nearly invisible to the naked eye. Only through long exposures and post-processing curve stretching were its icy nucleus and the magnificent tail, swept out by the solar wind, finally revealed.

As a typical Oort Cloud object, every visit it makes serves as a precious transmission of information regarding the early evolution of the Solar System.
